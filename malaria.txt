Disease Name: Malaria

Overview:
Malaria is a life-threatening disease caused by parasites transmitted through the bites of infected female Anopheles mosquitoes. It is most common in tropical and subtropical regions.

Causes:
Malaria is caused by Plasmodium parasites. Transmission occurs through mosquito bites, blood transfusion, or from mother to child during pregnancy.

Symptoms:
Symptoms include fever, chills, sweating, headache, nausea, vomiting, fatigue, and muscle pain. Symptoms usually appear 10–15 days after infection.

Prevention:
Preventive strategies include using insecticide-treated bed nets, indoor spraying, eliminating mosquito breeding sites, and early diagnosis and treatment.

Complications:
Severe malaria can cause anemia, organ failure, coma, and death if untreated.

When to Seek Medical Help:
Medical attention is necessary if fever develops after visiting malaria-prone regions.
