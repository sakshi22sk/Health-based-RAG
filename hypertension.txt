Disease Name: Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)

Overview:
Hypertension is a condition in which the pressure of blood against artery walls remains consistently high. It is often referred to as a “silent killer” because it may not cause noticeable symptoms.

Causes:
Hypertension can result from genetic factors, unhealthy diet, excessive salt intake, lack of physical activity, obesity, stress, and excessive alcohol consumption.

Symptoms:
Most individuals experience no symptoms. In severe cases, symptoms may include headaches, dizziness, chest pain, or shortness of breath.

Prevention:
Preventive measures include reducing salt intake, engaging in regular physical activity, maintaining a healthy weight, managing stress, and avoiding tobacco use.

Complications:
Uncontrolled hypertension increases the risk of heart disease, stroke, kidney damage, and vision problems.

When to Seek Medical Help:
Regular blood pressure monitoring is important, especially for adults over 30 or those with risk factors.
