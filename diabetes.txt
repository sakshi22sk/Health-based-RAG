Disease Name: Diabetes

Overview:
Diabetes is a chronic metabolic disorder characterized by high levels of blood glucose. It occurs when the body either does not produce enough insulin or cannot effectively use the insulin it produces.

Types:
The main types include Type 1 diabetes, Type 2 diabetes, and gestational diabetes. Type 2 diabetes is the most common and is often linked to lifestyle factors.

Symptoms:
Common symptoms include frequent urination, excessive thirst, increased hunger, fatigue, blurred vision, slow-healing wounds, and unexplained weight loss.

Risk Factors:
Risk factors include family history, obesity, physical inactivity, unhealthy diet, increasing age, and high blood pressure.

Prevention:
Type 2 diabetes can often be prevented or delayed through healthy eating, regular physical activity, maintaining a healthy weight, and routine health checkups.

Complications:
If left unmanaged, diabetes can lead to heart disease, kidney failure, nerve damage, vision loss, and other serious health problems.

When to Seek Medical Help:
Medical advice should be sought if symptoms persist or blood sugar levels remain consistently high.
