Disease Name: Tuberculosis (TB)

Overview:
Tuberculosis is a contagious infectious disease that mainly affects the lungs but can also affect other parts of the body. It spreads through the air when an infected person coughs or sneezes.

Causes:
TB is caused by Mycobacterium tuberculosis bacteria. Prolonged exposure to an infected person increases the risk of transmission.

Symptoms:
Common symptoms include persistent cough lasting more than two weeks, chest pain, coughing up blood, weight loss, fever, night sweats, and fatigue.

Prevention:
Preventive measures include early detection, completing prescribed treatment, BCG vaccination, and ensuring good ventilation.

Complications:
Untreated TB can cause severe lung damage and spread to other organs.

When to Seek Medical Help:
Medical consultation is required if cough persists for more than two weeks.
